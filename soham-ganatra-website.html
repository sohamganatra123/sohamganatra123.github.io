My conviction

Board thesis

01
Margin resilience is structural
Sustainable margin is not created through reactive cost cutting. It is built by embedding self-service architecture, automation, and cost-to-serve accountability into the operating model before pressure arrives.
02
Boards must audit the operating model
Beyond topline growth, boards should evaluate whether management has embedded the right governance mechanisms — AI-enabled efficiency, clear ownership of input metrics, and systems that improve operating leverage over time.
03
EBITDA durability is an output
Disciplined capital allocation and innovation investments tied to measurable economic impact are the drivers. In the boardroom, I prioritise structural efficiency over short-term expansion and challenge management to prove the economics of every initiative.
"Sustainable enterprise value comes from systems that scale in both growth cycles and downturns."

Board focus

Where I add most value

SaaS businesses PE-backed digital platforms Marketplace & e-commerce Growth stage companies Transformation mandates
I
Scale with cost discipline
I push management teams to demonstrate that their operating model has built-in leverage — asking whether growth adds proportional cost or whether automation and self-serve architecture are doing the heavy lifting. Headcount ratios, cost-to-serve trends, and automation coverage are the metrics I track.
II
AI & automation governance
I bring a practitioner's lens to AI adoption — having shipped demand forecasting, OCR automation, and self-serve AI bots. In the boardroom, I help management tie AI investment to measurable economic outcomes and build the right governance for responsible deployment.
III
Platform resilience & liquidity
For marketplace and SaaS platforms, I assess whether supplier adoption, platform availability, and self-serve coverage are strong enough to sustain performance through both demand spikes and downturns — the resilience indicators that growth metrics alone miss.
